Hidden Gems Info

Explore the details of the hidden gems in Toronto.

St. Lawrence Market

History: St. Lawrence Market has been serving the community since the 19th century. Originally established as a farmer's market, it has evolved into a culinary destination.

Directions: To get to St. Lawrence Market, you can take the subway to Union Station and walk east. If you're driving, head towards Front Street and look for parking nearby.

Suggestions: While at the market, be sure to try the peameal bacon sandwiches and explore the various food stalls offering international cuisine.

Graffiti Alley

History: Graffiti Alley gained popularity in the 2000s as local artists started using the space as a canvas for creative expression. It's now a recognized spot for street art enthusiasts.

Directions: Graffiti Alley is located off Queen Street West. If you're taking the streetcar, hop on the 501 and get off at Spadina Avenue.

Suggestions: Take your time exploring the vibrant artwork and snap some photos to capture the unique pieces. You might also discover trendy cafes and boutiques nearby.

High Park Cherry Blossoms

History: The tradition of cherry blossoms in High Park dates back to 1959 when the Japanese ambassador donated 2000 sakura trees. The annual blooming attracts visitors from all over.

Directions: High Park is located in the west end of Toronto. You can take the subway to High Park station or drive and use the available parking lots.

Suggestions: Plan your visit during the peak bloom period in late April to early May. Enjoy a leisurely stroll under the blossoms and consider bringing a picnic.

Evergreen Brick Works

History: The Evergreen Brick Works site was once a quarry and brick-making factory. It's now a sustainable community hub with trails, gardens, and educational programs.

Directions: Take public transit to Broadview station and then the free shuttle bus to the Brick Works. If driving, there's limited on-site parking available.

Suggestions: Explore the walking trails along the Don Valley, visit the farmer's market on weekends, and participate in eco-friendly workshops and events.
